Health Services

The Department of Health (DOH) works to protect and improve the health of all Hawaii residents.

Available Services

  • Vital Records — birth, death, marriage, and divorce certificates
  • Immunization records and COVID-19 resources
  • Behavioral Health Administration services
  • Women, Infants & Children (WIC) nutrition program
  • Environmental health inspections and permits
  • Communicable disease prevention

Employment & Labor

The Department of Labor and Industrial Relations (DLIR) provides workforce services statewide.

Unemployment Insurance

If you've lost your job through no fault of your own, you may be eligible for unemployment benefits.

  • File a new claim at ui.hawaii.gov
  • Weekly benefit amount: Up to $765
  • Duration: Up to 26 weeks
  • Must actively seek employment each week

Workforce Development

  • American Job Centers on Oahu, Maui, Kauai, and Hawaii Island
  • Skills training and career counseling
  • On-the-Job Training (OJT) programs
  • Youth employment programs (ages 14-24)

Housing Assistance

The Hawaii Public Housing Authority (HPHA) manages affordable housing programs statewide.

Programs

  • Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher Program — Rental assistance for low-income families
  • Federal Public Housing — 5,300+ units across the state
  • State Public Housing — Managed through county offices
  • Emergency Rental Assistance — Currently closed
  • Homeownership Programs — Down payment and closing cost assistance
Wait List Notice: The Section 8 wait list is currently closed. Sign up for email notifications when it reopens.

Education

Hawaii is the only state with a single, unified public school system under the Department of Education (DOE).

  • 256 public schools across 15 complex areas
  • 11 public charter schools
  • University of Hawaii system (10 campuses)
  • Hawaii P-20 Partnerships for Education pipeline
